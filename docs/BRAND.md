# Shaka Brand Notes

Shaka Workbench should feel like a practical developer instrument: local, precise, South African, and built for real work on a laptop.

## Product Position

- South African laptop-first developer agent.
- Developer workbench, not a chatbot skin.
- Local-first by default, cloud-capable when useful.
- Approval-aware autonomy instead of uncontrolled automation.
- Clear enough for contributors and portfolio reviewers to understand quickly.

## UI Principles

- **Workbench first:** prioritize files, plans, approvals, tasks, evals, and connector status over decorative chat chrome.
- **Readable under pressure:** use strong hierarchy, compact panels, and clear labels for state, risk, and next action.
- **Shared mental model:** CLI, TUI, and dashboard should use the same vocabulary for tasks, approvals, skills, connectors, and evals.
- **Local confidence:** show what is running locally, what touches external services, and what needs approval.
- **No gimmicks:** avoid mascots, novelty animations, fake terminal noise, or unclear cyberpunk styling.

## Logo Direction

Preferred mark: a compact shield, cursor, and node system.

- **Shield:** safety, approval gates, and controlled execution.
- **Cursor:** developer workflow and direct manipulation.
- **Node:** connectors, skills, and local runtime graph.

The mark should work as a small app icon, monochrome stamp, and dashboard header logo. It should remain geometric and simple, with enough distinctiveness to avoid generic AI sparkle or mascot language.

## Visual Tone

- Grounded, technical, and confident.
- Minimal copy, no hype-heavy claims.
- Strong contrast and purposeful spacing.
- South African identity can appear through language, examples, and restrained visual cues rather than flags or cliches.

## Avoid

- Mascots or anthropomorphic characters.
- Magic-wand, sparkle, or generic AI brain icons.
- Overpromising full autonomy.
- Decorative complexity that competes with code, approvals, or task state.
